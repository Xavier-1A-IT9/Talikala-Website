Dignity or Survival

"Dignity or survival, which should I choose? Each one has battles, I would lose. Desperate warrior as I am, food I need to provide for us to survive. What should I decide? Created using scrunched paper, and a pen."

"Her body has been tainted by these filthy hands, the same hands that give her what she needs. She hates it but she keeps going back. If it's for survival, she'll do anything. Created using paint, hands.."

The Other Woman

"What one can see and know about themselves, hidden from the views of others. A classic symbolism of what is hidden behind the pretty facade. A little secret. Created using scrap papers, brush, paint, and pencil."

Drowning in the Sea of Pressure

"A boy who is controlled by a societal pressure manifested as a monster. The pressure to do good, the pressure to exceed expectations. Drowning under the sea of pressure, he's unable to swim back up. All his cries and pleas are for naught for no one can hear him in the sea of pressure. Created using (materials such as pencil, brushes, paints, scrap papers, etc.)."

"As free as a bird"

As free as a bird, our minds unblurred. Free from the herd, this is what we deserve. Threads bind us forevermore, trapped in this never-ending cycle, Talikala's arrival sets us loose for we were misused. Created using a pencil, and color pencils."

At The End of The Rainbow

"Even after the heaviest of rains, or the rockiest of roads, our will shall not corrode. we shall overcome them all for the hope trudges on. The end of the rainbow is near, happiness is here. So move on, although the journey was tough and long, we will remain strong. Created using color pencils/markers, oil paints, colored papers and pencil."

Peace and growth

"A barren wasteland we live in no more. Flowers and trees are as far as the eyes can see. The hair flies in the direction of tranquility, finally away from all the cruelty. My life was destined to be doomed, but now I'm in these grassy lands where flowers bloom. Created using paint, paper, magazine cut-outs, and brushes."

  

Angeline Noreen S. Tamisin is one of the five members of Group 1A of 9-Xavier. She was born on October 11, 2007, and is diligent and efficient. She excels in speaking and organizing school works accordingly. Doing good in academics, she is also a proactive student leader being an ASEC member for three years. She is the current Grade 9 Representative and former Grade 8 and 7 Representative as well as the Class Secretary. Angie, what she liked to be called, not only finds joy in getting good grades but also in listening to music, reading books, watching shows, and spending time with her friends. Often multitasking with academics, extracurriculars, and fun, Angeline still makes sure that she gets things done.
